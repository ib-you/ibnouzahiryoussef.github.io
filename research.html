Research

PhD Thesis: Monetary Policy in the Context of Central Bank Independence: An Econometric Study

  • In-depth analysis of macroeconomic policies, including fiscal and monetary interventions.
  • Development of advanced models to assess the effectiveness of monetary policy in various economic contexts.
  • Empirical analysis of monetary policy frameworks, transmission mechanisms, and central bank responses to economic shocks, and their implications for inflation control and macroeconomic stability.
  • Evaluation of monetary policy instruments in promoting macroeconomic stability and managing inflation.
  • Analysis of legal and de facto central bank independence and its role in strengthening policy credibility and effectiveness.

Published Papers

Ibnouzahir, Y., & Hefnaoui, A. (2026). Shockwaves abroad and the vulnerability of monetary policy: Morocco’s central bank response to geopolitical shocks and commodity price volatility. This paper investigates the transmission mechanisms of geopolitical risk and commodity price volatility to Morocco’s monetary policy using a Bayesian VAR model (1995–2025). Results show persistent inflationary effects via imported inflation channels and highlight delayed policy responses, emphasizing the need for fiscal coordination.

Ibnouzahir, Y., & Hefnaoui, A. (2026). De Jure or De Facto Central Bank Independence? What Truly Curbs Inflation in Morocco. Using ARDL modeling and data from 1988–2024, this study finds that legal independence significantly reduces long-term monetary expansion, while governor turnover affects short-term dynamics, providing key insights for policy design in emerging economies.

Ibnouzahir, Y., et al. (2026). Okun’s Law and Phillips Curve Revisited: Nonlinear Evidence from the Moroccan Economy. Based on NARDL models (1981–2024), the study reveals asymmetric relationships and confirms structural rigidities, supporting policies aimed at labor-intensive investment and macroeconomic resilience.

Hefnaoui, A., & Ibnouzahir, Y. (2024). Effectiveness of Monetary Policy after Inflation Targeting: The Case of Bank Al-Maghrib. Using a VAR model (2007–2023), findings show that inflation is mainly driven by exchange rate and money supply shocks, with limited policy rate impact, suggesting the need for structural reforms.
